Concept 1: Arctic Fox Prey Floor

For my Enrichment project earlier this semester, I did extensive research about the arctic fox. I was very interested in the animal and would look forward to continuing to work on this animal for my final project. This project would be centered around enrichment for arctic foxes that are in a zoo environment who don't often get the opportunity to hunt for prey and exercise their unique ability to pin-point the location of prey underneath layers of snow using their keen sense of sound and natural compass. I seek to produce a platform floor that the arctix fox would be able to approach during feeding time and then pounce to stop a sound that was playing underneath the floor in order to obtain the food. More details about the mechanics can be seen in Week 5.

NYC Owls (Saw-Whet & Eastern Screech) and Captive Breeding Programs

Researcher Robert DeCandido, PhD, and Animal Photographer Deborah Allen have been working on conservation and research around the owls in NYC. Since 1998, there has been a program in Central Park to release more owls into the wild setting. At a time, they were able to successfully release 33 owls that bred and reproduced after being introduced to Central Park. Reasons for their decline cited include habitat loss, pestocide poisoning, and car collisions to a lesser extent.

One breeding program was told by the state of New York that they were not able to continue with their conservation and release efforts due to a lack of proof that the program was working. They cite the only way in which they will be able to prove it is working is if owl sightings are increased. Therefore, I wonder if I can create something that would attract owls to areas closer to humans where they have a greater chance of being seen (without being harmed of course) and therefore a greater chance for the species to survive in this region of the United States.
